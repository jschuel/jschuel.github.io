Teaching

Teaching Statement

Prior to pursuing a career in academia, I taught high school physics for two years through the New York City Teaching Fellows program at Maspeth High School in Queens, NY, developing hands-on laboratory experiences and mentoring students in advanced mechanics and electromagnetism.

I strive to incorporate student-centered learning in every class. While at the University of Hawaiʻi, I and two fellow graduate students piloted a recitation program for our calculus-based Physics I courses. Each week, one lecture was replaced by a graduate-student–led problem session in which students worked in small groups on context-rich problems, with the instructor acting as a facilitator through guided questioning. This initiative proved so effective that the department now includes these sessions in all undergraduate physics courses.

Building on that success, I introduced recitation sessions in upper-division quantum mechanics classes, enabling deeper conceptual engagement and peer instruction. These sessions have since become a regular component of UH’s curriculum.
